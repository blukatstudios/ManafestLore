History

The Beginning

Europa began as a sprawling wilderness inhabited by primitive tribes. When the divines came, they used Europa as an arena to pit their followers against one another. Dissatisfied with mere human followers, the divines created new races to fight on their behalf. The goblins, demons, and orcs soon outnumbered humans in the region.

Enlightened Dreams

Some dreamers came from another realm to escape a civil war. They sought out the safety through obscurity by hiding with the scattered human tribes. In exchange for their safety they shared their dreams of an alternative history and its machines. The tribes gathered together and dragged themselves through an industrial revolution toward the hope of safety through technological superiority. The state of Europa was born with steel, gunpowder, and coal.

Arcane Apex

The in-fighting of divine armies halted when the humans claimed several strategic locations and held them against repeated attacks. From the West came the demons, from the North the goblins, and from the East came orcs with the common goal of crushing the human resistance. The development of repeating rifles, artillery, and trench warfare held the borders. Dumbstruck by the effectiveness of Europan weapons, Carthage began to trade arcane technologies for mundane weapons.

Divine Annihilation

Europa very quickly learned to convert the energy of radioactive materials into mana. By trading mana for supplies and more arcane technologies, Europa weathered the war and began development of a weapon to kill a divine with. After the first field test of the bomb against an army of orcs, the divines built up for an assault they would lead. A combination of divine magic and the detonation of severalal god-killers resulted in the destruction of both sides. Magical beasts flocked to the ruins to bask in the area's divine fallout. Europa and Persia blockaded their borders with Europa to keep the refugees and monsters from the area out. Though many Europan devices remained, their underlying technologies were lost under the rubble of collapsed factories and laboratories.

Europan Dark Age

The abundant source of mana from Europa disappeared, crippling the mana-based industries that had grown dependent upon it. Carthage nearly collapsed from the resulting depression. Europa's government was reborn in a walled-off section of intact cityscape named Oasis. Though strong enough to fight off raiders and monsters, this new Europan state was a shadow of its former self. Europa's ruins became the home of migrant gauls, roman treasure hunters, and exiles world round thanks to Carthage's portal nexus.

Military

At its height Europa was armed to the teeth with a variety of different experimental technologies. In exchange for selling all their findings with Carthage, they gained access to food, metals, and facilities for their research efforts outside of the war zone.
The cubes were massive military fortresses erected with the goal of temporarily housing civilians during the evacuation of warzones. As defensive battles recurred on a near-weekly basis, these structures filled to the brim with civilian refugees. Every window became a micro garden, every bedroom a home for multiple families. The need for space grew dire enough to justify sending refugees down into the mines where they erected shacks from scrap materials.
At the center of each cube was a nuclear reactor that produced all the mana needed to light the entire structure even into the mines and networks of tunnels that the excess population overflowwed into. A powerful anti artillery force shield provided residents with safety from long range attacks, whilst a small arsennal of mana-powered artillery on the roof supplied firepower to engage distant enemies. Once the reinforced doors and windows were breached, however, the structure would serve as a honeycomb of tight corridors to be used as defensive positions.
Trench warfare became the main strategy of Europan defense. Gobelins, demons, and orcs would charge across flat lands into suppressive fire and artillery whilst sharpshooters took deadly aim. Whilst this was largely successful, subsequent attacks were rarely from the same angle or at the same time of day.
Experimental aircraft dropped mines over heavily-traveled areas whilst dropping bombs on suspected staging grounds. When successful, these air raids pre-emptively disorganized the enemy before an attack could arrive. The tactical insertion of infantry by these aircraft allowed for the capture of strategic resources such as weapons, clothing, and food. Powered by mana, this small fleet provided round-the-clock air support for Europan forces.
