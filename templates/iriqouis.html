History

Divine intervention

The Iriquois were experiencing an extended period of peace at the time of the divine arrival. The scarcely-populated homeland was overlooked by power-seeking divines, but became the home of the nature goddess. She befriended fae, human, and dragon alike when she came bearing gifts for all. After a very cursory survey of the humans in the region, she offered her services in transformation.
The nature goddess turned willing worshippers into half-beast creatures. By giving up their pure human form they gained endurance, strength, claws, jaws, and a heightened set of senses. Feared at first, the transformation ceremony was quickly accepted due to the shear utility of such advantages in hunter-gatherer society. Not only did the transformation aid in survival, but compatible half-beasts rendered more children(with twins and triplets being the rule, rather than the exception) that matured faster than their full human counterparts. The half-beast population boomed and spread out across the land.
As the half-beasts spreaded south, the Aztecs quickly learned of their existence and send professional armies to capture and enslave half-beasts. In reaction to the slaving, the New Iriqouis were formed as a federation of tribes.
