Mundane Technology

A wide variety of technologies rely solely upon aspects of nature devoid of magic. These are thus names mundane arts and sciences. Below are different categories of technology broken up by their epochs.

Epoch: Stone Age

Leveraging non-metalic materials such as bone, wood, and flint, tools were fashioned as labor multipliers and powerful weapons against animals.

Construction

  • Hut
  • Tent
  • Cottage
  • Mud

Industry

  • Hunting(Spear, Bow)
  • Forestry(Axe)
  • Pottery
  • Agriculture
  • Boats

Military

  • Spear
  • Bow
  • Domestication

Epoch: Metal Age

The refining of metals such as copper and iron as well as the manufacturing of more advanced metals such as bronze and steel allow stronger, more durable traditional tools. Muzzle-loaded firearms become possible with these new materials.

Construction

  • Brick
  • Stone
  • Plank
  • Paved roads
  • Sewers
  • Windows

Industry

  • Mining
  • Advanced Forestry(Saw)
  • Carpentry
  • Cement
  • Stone Working
  • Workshop
  • Glass and Metal containers
  • Sea Ships

Military

  • Pike
  • Knife
  • Sword
  • Muzzle loaders(Musket, Rifle, blunderbus)
  • Repeaters(Harmonica, revolver, chain)
  • Explosives

Epoch: Steam Age

The steam age leverages metal and machining to harness the power of compressed steam. The resulting steam engine allows for significant advances in both transportation and industrial processes. The standardization of parts allows machining to be easily serviced by replacing bad parts for good ones.

Construction

  • Metal supports
  • Rails

Industry

  • Factory
  • Steam-powered tooling
  • Mass Production

Military

  • Repeater(lever, bolt, rotary)
  • Break-action
  • Metal Cartridges
  • Trench warfare

Epoch: Oil Age

As machining and chemistry advanced further, oil was refined for use in internal combustion engines. These compact, efficient devices coincided with the development of lighter and more durable materials required for automobiles, automatic weapons, and portable electrical generators.

Construction

  • Electric lighting

Industry

  • Radio
  • Electricity-powered tooling
  • Automobiles

Military

  • Repeater(Auto)
  • Airplane
  • Armored vehicle(Transport, Tank, Artillery)
