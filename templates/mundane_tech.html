Mundane Technology

A wide variety of technologies rely solely upon aspects of nature separate from the exploitation of mana or arcane materials. These mundane arts and sciences are the foundation of all applied magic tecnology. Mundane tech can be classified in a tier of epochs. Though a rough hierarchy of these epochs might suggest a linear progression from simpler to more advanced epochs, it is not that simple, particularly when trade and availability of resources are taken into consideration.

Stone

The eponymous commodity of the stone age are the blades, hammers, and anvils made from stone. Mud, clay, and unprocessed lumber are most popular construction materials due to their availability and relative ease of construction. Harnessing fire allows the firing of earthenware capable of storing dry and wet goods.

Metal

With plentiful fuel and ore came the first attempts at metallurgy. Bronze stood above most other metals, and iron above it. The large-scale production of stone bricks became feasible during this epoch, as did heavy body armor and shields.

Steam

Imrovements in metallurgy led to steels capable of enduring the high pressures required to build firearms, rendering heavy body armor obsolete. The advanced metallurgy also led to the development of machines powered by steam pressure. In particular, trains offered relatively cheap and fast transportation of large quantities of goods.

Oil

Further developments in chemistry led to the internal combustion engine powered by alcohol and more commonly petroleum. Man-portable tools such as chainsaws and pneumatic drills allowed greater industrial output than ever before. Factories with lines of highly-specialized tooling turned out goods in great volumes. Likewise man-portable firearms evolved to self-load from magazines, leading to semi and fully automatic firearms. Trench warfare quickly rendered open warfare obsolete. Airplanes were the highest development of the time, capable of moving very quickly across great distances.
